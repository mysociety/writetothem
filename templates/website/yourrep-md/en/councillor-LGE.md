# When should I contact a Local Government District Councillor?

Local Government District Councils in Northern Ireland are responsible for a range of local services. Your Councillor can help with issues including:

- **Planning** and local development
- **Waste management** and recycling
- **Community planning** and development
- **Leisure and recreation** facilities
- **Environmental health** and licensing
- **Building control** and regulation
- **Local economic development** and tourism

For issues such as education, health, roads, housing, and social services, you should contact your Northern Ireland Assembly Member instead, as these are the responsibility of the Northern Ireland Executive.

You have multiple councillors representing you.
You can chose to write to one of them, or send a message to all of them.

For a personal matter, you might prefer to write to a single councillor. If you write to multiple councillors, this will be clear in your message and they might collectively just send one response. 

Don’t worry if you’re not absolutely sure who to pick! Whoever you write to should forward your letter to the most appropriate person, if you haven’t got it quite right.
