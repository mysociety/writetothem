# When should I contact a District Councillor?

District Councils are one of two tiers of local government in some parts of England. Your District Councillor can help with issues handled by the district council, including:

- **Council housing** and housing policy
- **Planning applications** and local development
- **Rubbish collection** and recycling
- **Local environment** issues such as parks, street cleaning and noise complaints
- **Council tax** collection and benefits
- **Administration of elections**

For issues such as education, transport and roads, fire services, social services, and libraries, you should contact your County Councillor instead.

Don't worry if you're not absolutely sure who to pick! Whoever you write to should forward your letter to the most appropriate person, if you haven't got it quite right.
