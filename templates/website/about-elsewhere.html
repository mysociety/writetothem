At the moment, WriteToThem.com can't directly get you in contact with elected representatives outside the United Kingdom. But we can try to help you get in touch by yourself:

The Falkland Islands

Eight representatives are elected to the Legislative Council of the Falkland Islands every four years, five from Stanley and three from the remainder of the Islands. The Falkland Islands government's web site is at http://www.falklands.gov.fk/; there's a list of the current members of the Council. We can't find an email address for contacting members of the Legislative Council, but you could try sending a request to admin@falklands.gov.fk.

South Georgia and the South Sandwich Islands

South Georgia and the South Sandwich Islands are governed by a Commissioner (who is also Governor of the Falklands) from the Falkland Islands. There are no elected representatives for the islands.

St Helena, Ascension Island, and Tristan da Cunha

The website of the government of St Helena is at http://www.sainthelena.gov.sh/. Twelve members are elected to a Legislative Council every four years; there is a list of the members of the Council and the Clerk of the Councils can be contacted at cofcouncils@sainthelena.gov.sh.

Ascension Island presently has no democratic representatives. There is an Administrator, and proposals to create an elected Islands Council. You can read more at http://www.ascension-island.gov.ac/.

Tristan da Cunha is governed by an Administrator, appointed by the Governor of St Helena. The Administrator is advised by an Island Council of eight elected members. We have no email contact details for the Island Council -- sorry. More information about the Tristan da Cunha Island Council.

British Antarctic Territory

The British Antarctic Territory has no elected representatives. You can read more at the website for the British Antarctic Survey.

Isle of Man

Twenty-four members are elected to the House of Keys, the lower house of the Tynwald, the Parliament of the Isle of Man. The current members of Keys; the constituencies from which the members are elected.

Jersey

The Jersey legislature is the States Assembly; you can see a list of its members with email addresses.

Guernsey

The Guernsey legislature is the States of Deliberation; there is a list of its members.

Gibraltar

There are fifteen members of Gibraltar's legislature, the House of Assembly, elected by popular vote. The Gibraltarian Parliament's site includes details of the current members of the House (at the bottom of the page); you can write to members at the address,

House of Assembly, 156 Main Street, Gibraltar

or contact them by phone on +350 78420, or by fax to +350 42849.

For the purposes of the EU, Gibraltar is considered part of the UK's South West region and so is represented by that region's 7 MEPs. Unfortunately you cannot contact them through this site yet, but here is a list of the South West UK MEPs, including contact details.

Turks and Caicos Islands

The Turks and Caicos Islands are a British Overseas Territory, with a Governor. The head of government is the Chief Minister. The Executive Council (their Cabinet) consists of three ex officio members and five appointed by the governor from among the members of the Legislative Council (parliament). The Legislative Council consists of 19 seats, of which 13 are popularly elected. There are contact details for the Ministers on the Government's web site.

Cayman Islands

The Cayman Islands are a British Overseas Territory, with a Governor. Daily administration of the Islands is conducted by the Executive Council, 5 of whose 8 members are elected by the Assembly. Elections to the 18-seat Assembly are usually every four years. Contact details for most members of the Assembly are given on the website of the Government of the Cayman Islands (follow the link for "Legislature (MLAs)" on the left).

Montserrat

Montserrat is a British Overseas Territory, with a Governor and an Executive Council (ExCo) responsible for day-to-day administration. Members of the ExCo are partly drawn from the members of the elected Legislative Council. There is information about ExCo members on the Montserrat government website, but no details for members of the Legislative Council.

Bermuda

Bermuda is a British Overseas Territory, with a Governor. The legislature consists of two houses: the elected House of Assembly, and an appointed Senate. Contact details are available on the website of the Government of Bermuda.
